Method and experiments

Decision kernel, probe, VOA policy, and evidence plan

The method is deliberately scoped as one decision kernel, one shared self-knowledge signal, and two auxiliaries: an edit-risk surrogate and a training-time user simulator.

Architecture

Estimate failure risk before committing to an edit.

DecideVOA policy

Choose the action with maximum expected value.

The shared probe is the scientific bridge between G1 and G3: it can trigger abstention when failure cost is high and serve as a risk surrogate when calculating whether asking has value.

Metrics

Silent Hallucination Rate

Fraction of infeasible requests where the system edits instead of choosing a justified non-edit action. Lower is better.

Over-Refusal Rate

Fraction of feasible twins where the system refuses or flags incorrectly. Lower is better.

Risk-Coverage AUC

Measures selective editing quality as coverage varies. Higher is better when high-risk edits are rejected first.

Brier Score

Proper scoring rule for calibrated failure probability. Primary calibration metric; ECE is secondary.

Net Utility

Cost-sensitive score over edit success, hallucination cost, refusal cost, clarification cost, and mask cost.

Twin Success

Whether the system can satisfy paired feasible or counterfactual examples without exploiting superficial wording.

Baselines and Comparisons

  • Trivial policies: always edit, always abstain, generic ask, direct edit.
  • Abstention baselines: text-only refusal classifiers, should-edit gates, SpotEdit-style target checks.
  • Clarification baselines: proactive clarification, information-gain question selection, fixed threshold asking.
  • Judge baselines: post-hoc VLM judge, random rejection, oracle risk, and oracle VOA.
  • Ablations: shared probe vs decoupled probes, graph vs no graph, cost model variants, feasible twin removal.
