EditDecide: image editing as a decision problem

We reframe instruction-based image editing from pure generation into cost-sensitive action selection under latent feasibility f and intent z. A trustworthy editor should decide whether to edit, abstain, flag, ask, confirm, or request a mask before producing a plausible but wrong image.

The exact proposal in one frame

Current editors execute instructions, but they rarely decide whether the request is feasible, clear, safe, or likely to fail. EditDecide makes that decision explicit.

Unified action space

The proposal is about deciding before editing

EDIT

Feasible, sufficiently clear, and expected risk is below the cost-sensitive threshold.

ABSTAIN

Information is unrecoverable or editor failure risk makes action worse than non-action.

FLAG

False premise, contradiction, missing executable context, or out-of-scope request.

ASK

Clarification has positive value after subtracting user cost.

CONFIRM

The likely intent is known but costly enough to require confirmation.

REQUEST_MASK

Language is inefficient and spatial input is the right disambiguation channel.

Scope lock

Taxonomy

Failure types the benchmark must separate

Five infeasibility types

false_premise impossible contradictory ill_posed out_of_scope_or_unsafe

These support silent hallucination measurement, over-refusal controls, reviewer defense, and pixel-grounded feasibility rather than text-only abstention.

Seven ambiguity types

target operation region degree style preservation interaction

These keep clarification in the unified schema while limiting main-paper scope to cached counterfactual twin evaluation.

Reviewer defense

Known attacks and planned evidence

"This is AbstentionBench for pixels."

Defense: pixel-grounded feasibility, feasible twins, over-refusal metrics, and editor-output evaluation.

"The unified story is decorative."

Defense: joint-VOA vs two-stage and shared-vs-decoupled probe are main-paper ablations.

"Synthetic data is unrealistic."

Defense: 100-150 human seed examples, independent labels, Cohen's kappa, and distribution tests.

"VLM judges drift or are unreliable."

Defense: pinned judge version, public prompt, judge-human agreement, and multiple-judge sensitivity.

"Self-knowledge cannot be tested on API editors."

Defense: explicitly split black-box G1/G4 editors from open-weight G2/probe editors.

Positioning

The paper should claim editing-specific instantiation, not new decision theory

VOA, reject options, and clarification value are known ideas. EditDecide's defensible novelty is the instruction-based image editing instantiation: pixel-grounded feasibility, feasible-twin controls, editor self-knowledge, and a single schema that tests joint action selection.
