Execution plan

Compute-constrained plan and release artifacts

The hard operational constraint is that all GPU-dependent experiments must be run and cached before August 15. After that date, work should be limited to writing, human validation, offline analysis, and figure regeneration from frozen caches.

Two-Phase Timeline

Phase A: now to August 15

W1

Lock scope, schema, five infeasibility types, seven ambiguity types, VOA formulation, cost model, and 20-30 high-quality examples.

W2-W3

Build S1, S1', S2, S3, S3', and R. Run initial judge-human agreement and distribution checks.

W4

Train EditDecide-Lite, cache all editor outputs, and produce the first SHR-ORR Pareto.

W5

Collect hidden states, train self-knowledge probes, report Brier and risk-coverage, and calibrate the surrogate.

W6-W7

Run VOA Rerank, Oracle-VOA, joint-vs-two-stage, shared-vs-decoupled, baseline comparisons, and twin outputs.

8/15

Freeze C1-C9 and verify every figure can be regenerated without GPU.

Phase B: August 15 to late September

8/15-8/22

Human evaluation correlation, final calibration, and failure taxonomy.

8/22-9/5

Counterfactual twin analysis, ablation write-up, and related-work comparison.

9/5-9/18

Draft the 9-page main paper, appendix, rebuttal bank, significance tests, and final figures.

9/18-deadline

Internal review, compression, OpenReview submission, and release preparation.

Go/No-Go Triggers

CheckpointMust be trueFallback
W3Benchmark quality and judge kappa are stable.Shrink to G1 single-track benchmark paper.
W4SOTA editors have meaningfully high silent hallucination rates.Move G1 to analysis and shift mainline to G2.
W5Risk-coverage beats random and C2-C4 run end-to-end.Limit G2 to single-editor probe or appendix.
W7VOA Rerank beats generic baselines and joint beats two-stage.Reframe as complementary evaluations plus shared signal.
Mid-SepPaper is focused, statistically defensible, and complete.Downgrade to workshop or benchmark-only release.
