Proposal

Project framing and paper proposal

EditDecide reframes instruction-guided image editing from a pure generation problem into a cost-sensitive decision problem under latent feasibility f and intent z. The editor should decide whether it should act before producing an image.

Motivation

Current instruction-based editors typically assume that the user request is executable, the intent is clear, and the correct edit is unique. In real use these assumptions fail systematically.

GapFailed assumptionBad behaviorExpected behavior
G1 feasibilityThe request is executableSilently edits an impossible or false-premise instructionABSTAIN, FLAG, or explain
G2 self-knowledgeThe editor can safely attempt the editCommits despite knowing signals of likely failureCalibrated failure confidence and selective editing
G3 clarificationUser intent is clearBlindly guesses the target, operation, degree, or regionASK, CONFIRM, or REQUEST_MASK
G4 disagreementThere is only one correct outputCollapses legal alternatives into one modeEvaluate valid edit distributions

The unified insight is that G1-G4 are different regions of the same belief-space decision problem. When feasibility is unlikely, refuse or flag. When feasibility is high but intent is uncertain, ask or request spatial input. When risk is low, edit.

Research Questions

  • RQ-G1: Can editors correctly reject infeasible, contradictory, false-premise, ill-posed, or unsafe edits without over-refusing feasible twins?
  • RQ-G2: Can editor failure be predicted from internal or pre-generation signals, and does a calibrated probe improve risk-coverage?
  • RQ-G3: Does clarification reduce edit risk after subtracting user cost, and when is a mask better than language?
  • RQ-G4: Do editors collapse valid multi-solution requests, and can distributional scoring better match human acceptability?
  • RQ-Unified: Does a joint value-of-action policy beat a two-stage filter-then-ask pipeline?

Scope Lock

Mainline

G1 feasibility and G2 self-knowledge are the paper backbone. Required evidence includes SHR-ORR Pareto, feasible twins, risk-coverage, Brier score, VOA Rerank, Oracle-VOA, joint vs two-stage, shared vs decoupled probe, and EditDecide-Lite.

Extension

G3 remains as a cacheable extension through counterfactual twins, direct-edit ceiling, and a small human seed slice. Full live do-ask intervention is not part of the mainline.

Deferred

G4 disagreement distribution, full multi-step human-in-loop clarification, and cost-sensitive GRPO are future work unless the mainline stabilizes early.

Contribution Claims

  1. Reframe instruction-based image editing as cost-sensitive decision-making under latent feasibility and intent.
  2. Introduce EditDecide-Bench, a shared image-pool and schema benchmark for abstention, self-knowledge, and clarification evaluation.
  3. Propose EditDecide: an intent-feasibility graph plus shared self-knowledge probe g(s) that drives abstention and serves as a VOI risk surrogate.
  4. Show improvements in silent hallucination, over-refusal trade-off, risk-coverage, net utility, and joint decision-making over two-stage baselines.

Claims should be domain-limited. The project should not claim new decision theory; the defensible novelty is the image-editing instantiation, pixel-grounded feasibility, feasible twins, self-knowledge calibration, and unified evaluation.
